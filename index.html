Learning With You connects with multicultural community Learning With You is designed to help members of the multicultural community connect with Chesterfield County Public Schools. This free, four-part program is part of CCPS on the Go! Participants will get an overview of the school division, then take a deeper dive into topics like budgeting, instruction, operations and long-range planning. This is an opportunity to develop an understanding of all the components of one of the largest school divisions in the nation. Each Learning With You gathering will take place 6-8:30 p.m. at the School Board Administration Building, located at 9900 Krause Road.

We Need You!

Next year's PTA now has just two vacant positions: Treasurer and 5K Committee Chair. The Treasurer is a vital role for the PTA. If we don't have one, the PTA won't be able to operate next year. The gig runs in tandem with the PTAs fiscal year: July 1 - June 30. If you are good with figures and believe in promoting the educational vitality of your child(ren) and SCES as a whole, please volunteer! We are currently in the process of planning this year's 5K. It's a team effort with Woolridge Elementary and Clover Hill Elementary. If you're interested in the position and want to get a front-row seat to see how it comes together before you take the role full-time, now is the perfect time to step up! Please contact Catherine Vacovsky at vp@swiftcreekpta.org or via Facebook Messenger if you're interested in either position.

Join us for the Reservoir Race Series on May 1st! Clover Hill Elementary, Swift Creek Elementary, and Woolridge Elementary have joined forces and created the Reservoir Run for 2022. Instead of three separate races, these schools will be hosting one large event on Sunday, May 1. This event includes a kids fun run at 2:00 pm as well as a 5k or 10k option that begins at 2:30 pm. We hope you will be a part of it! The purpose of this race is not only to bring together three neighboring school communities, but to also raise money for each school. This money will allow the schools to further enhance school enrichment with items such as new technology and equipment, health and wellness programs, staff conference sponsorships, and children's engineering programs, which will benefit students and teachers alike.
